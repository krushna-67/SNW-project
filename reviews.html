Bhel

A light and tangy snack made from puffed rice, sev, chutneys, and vegetables. Pune’s street-style bhel perfectly balances sweet, spicy, and crunchy flavors.

Pros: Fresh ingredients, well-balanced taste, easy to share.
Cons: Gets soggy quickly if not eaten immediately.

Bhel from Kalyan Bhel on Karve Road is a must-try for every Punekar. The mix of tamarind and mint chutney adds a unique local twist that’s hard to find elsewhere.

Pani Puri

Crispy puris filled with spicy, tangy, and sweet flavored water — the ultimate Indian street snack. Pune’s pani puri vendors often offer a mix of masala water styles.

Pros: Bursting with flavor, very affordable, fun to eat.
Cons: Can get messy; hygiene depends on the stall.

Try the Pani Puri at JM Road’s Shubham Chaat Center. The crispiness of the puris and the balance between spicy and sweet water make it stand out.

Vada Pav

Often called the “Indian burger,” this classic Maharashtrian snack has a spicy potato filling deep-fried in gram flour batter, sandwiched inside a pav with chutneys.

Pros: Spicy, filling, authentic local flavor.
Cons: High in oil; varies in quality by vendor.

JJ Garden Vada Pav near Camp is a legend. The crisp vada and soft pav, paired with their garlic chutney, create an unbeatable taste.

Pav Bhaji

A thick vegetable curry served with buttered pav (bread rolls). It’s rich, spicy, and bursting with flavor — a must-have at any Pune food stall.

Pros: Deliciously buttery, good portion size, full meal.
Cons: Heavy dish, not ideal for late-night eating.

Try the Pav Bhaji at DP Road’s Relax Pav Bhaji Stall. It’s known for its generous butter topping and smoky masala flavor.
